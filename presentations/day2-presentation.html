The Problem

Three layers of pain.

External

Costs too much, delivers too little

Premiums rise every year. Same treatment costs 40-70% more in a hospital than an independent practice. 8% of people drive 80% of the spending.

Internal

Employers feel powerless

Renewal season is a painful exercise in choosing the least-bad option. Brokers are commoditized — every competitor has the same products.

Philosophical

Every company deserves Fortune 100 benefits

The size of your company shouldn't determine the quality of your employees' care. Your team works just as hard.

Core Differentiator

The "Our Town" Model

This is what makes Vxtra structurally different from everything else in the market.

Local Employers

Self-insured companies (200-2,000 employees) in the same secondary market pool together for collective purchasing power.

Vxtra Health

Orchestrates the marketplace: aggregates risk, connects stakeholders, powers everything with Capital Rx technology.

Local Physicians

"People like us do things like this." — In secondary markets, the dots are closer together. Everyone knows everyone. Brand equity already exists in the community. You don't need a national campaign — you need the right 5 physician influencers and 5 employers.

Messaging Rules

What we say. What we never say.

Always Use

"Physician" or "Doctor"
"Educate without offending"
"Our money" (not "my money")
"Avoidable cost" (not fraud/waste)
"Agency" (for physicians)
"Our Town" (marketplace concept)
"People like us do things like this"
Dollar amounts, not % — "$25/employee/month" > "25% savings"

Never Use

"Provider" — "They're not freaking providers. They're physicians."
"Healthcare is broken" — "We'll never say healthcare is broken."
"It's Just Better" — Not clear enough.
Insurance jargon — EOB, TPA, PBM, CPT, NADAC, stop-loss
High cognitive load — "The lower the load, the more people act."

Let's Create...

Brand assets and messaging framework — all based on Day 1 decisions.

One-Liners

Problem, Solution, Outcome — by audience.

1. The Employer

Problem: Most self-insured employers are paying more every year for health plans designed for insurance company profits, not their people.

Solution: Vxtra Health gives employers with 200 to 2,000 employees a physician-led health plan powered by the same technology Fortune 100 companies use — with real-time data, local physicians, and costs you can actually see and control.

Outcome: Your employees get better care from doctors they trust, your costs go down by hundreds of dollars per employee per month, and you become the leader who finally fixed healthcare for your company.

2. The Broker

Problem: Every broker in town is selling the same carriers and the same products — and 60% are now owned by private equity that won't innovate.

Solution: Vxtra Health gives independent brokers an exclusive, physician-led health plan backed by Fortune 100 technology that your competitors don't have access to — built specifically for the self-funded employers you already serve.

Outcome: Your clients save real money, your book of business is protected, and employers come to you because you're the one with the plan that actually works.

One-Liners

Problem, Solution, Outcome — continued.

3. The Physician

Problem: Insurance companies have stripped physicians of their agency — burying them in pre-authorizations, denials, and administrative overhead instead of letting them practice medicine.

Solution: Vxtra Health puts physicians back in charge of patient care, working directly with local employers and their employees, supported by technology that handles claims and data so doctors can focus on medicine.

Outcome: Your patients trust you, not a call center. You make the clinical decisions. And you run an independent practice in a community that knows your name.

4. The Employee

Problem: Healthcare is confusing and way too expensive — and most people dread going to the doctor because they're afraid of what it'll cost.

Solution: Your employer has partnered with Vxtra Health to connect you with local physicians who actually know you — with lower out-of-pocket costs and none of the insurance runaround.

Outcome: You call your doctor, you get an appointment, you get the care you need, and you don't dread opening the bill.

BrandScript

1. The Employer (Self-Funded, 200–2,000 Employees)

Character

A self-insured employer tired of annual renewal shock — unable to give their team the benefits they deserve at a price they can control.

Want: Fortune 100-level benefits at a controllable cost — and hero status to their board.

Problem

External: Health insurance costs too much and delivers too little

Internal: Feels powerless every renewal cycle — choosing between cutting benefits or absorbing unsustainable costs

Philosophical: Every company deserves Fortune 100 benefits

Guide

Empathy: "We know what renewal season feels like."

Authority: Capital Rx (Fortune 100 technology, 7-day data), Tokyo Marine HCC ($1.5M invested), 30+ years physician-led experience

Plan

1. Schedule a conversation  →  2. Get a custom plan design  →  3. Take control of your health plan

CTA: "Schedule a Call"

Failure

Costs double in a decade. 68% cite benefits as reason to leave. Employees avoid care — small problems become expensive emergencies.

Success

Predictable, lower costs. Real-time data visibility. Employees say "my employer cares about me." Hero status to the board.

Transformation: From dreading renewal season → Confident in predictable costs. From powerless → Leading the change.

BrandScript

2. The Broker (Independent Benefits Advisor)

Character

An independent broker competing where 60% of peers are PE-owned, selling the exact same products as everyone else.

Want: A product no one else can offer that secures existing business and wins new clients.

Problem

External: Every health plan looks the same

Internal: Commoditized — differentiation is impossible with the same carriers

Philosophical: Independent brokers should be able to compete and win

Guide

Empathy: "It's exhausting trying to stand out with the same products as everyone else."

Authority: Exclusive access to Capital Rx Fortune 100 technology — 7-day data vs. 120-day industry standard. Built for the employers brokers already serve.

Plan

1. Have a conversation  →  2. Get exclusive plan access  →  3. Win and retain clients

CTA: "Schedule a Call"

Failure

Lose clients to PE-backed firms. No differentiation. Revenue stagnates. Independence is threatened.

Success

The only product in the market no competitor can offer. Existing business secured. New business pipeline full. Independence protected.

Transformation: From competing with the same products → Offering something no one else has. From vulnerable → Protected.

BrandScript

3. The Physician

Character

A physician buried in pre-authorizations, denials, and insurance bureaucracy — spending more time fighting paperwork than practicing medicine.

Want: Agency restored. The ability to practice medicine the way they were trained.

Problem

External: Insurance companies control clinical decisions

Internal: Lost their professional agency — told how to treat patients by people who've never seen them

Philosophical: Physicians should practice medicine, not fight insurance companies

Guide

Empathy: "You didn't spend a decade in training to fight pre-auth forms."

Authority: Larry grew a urology practice from 30 to 100 doctors. Sandra's 30+ years in health plan operations.

Plan

1. Join the Vxtra network  →  2. Work directly with local employers  →  3. Practice medicine your way

CTA: "Join the Network"

Failure

More pre-authorizations. Less autonomy. Hospital systems buying up independent practices. 80% of hospital-employed physicians want out.

Success

Agency restored. No pre-auths, no denials. Community-centered care. Independent practice in a community that knows your name.

Transformation: From fighting insurance bureaucracy → Making clinical decisions. From losing independence → Running your own practice.

BrandScript

4. The Employee (End Beneficiary)

Character

An employee confused by healthcare, afraid of costs, dreading the doctor because they don't know what it'll cost them.

Want: Healthcare that works without the stress, confusion, or financial fear.

Problem

External: Healthcare is confusing and way too expensive

Internal: Dreads going to the doctor — afraid of what it'll cost

Philosophical: Everyone deserves affordable, accessible care

Guide

Empathy: "Nobody should dread going to the doctor because they're afraid of what it'll cost."

Authority: Your employer partnered with Vxtra. Personal care navigation. $0 copays, $0 deductibles.

Plan

1. Get connected to your physician  →  2. Use your benefits without barriers  →  3. Get the care you need

CTA: "Meet Your Care Team"

Failure

Avoids care. Small problems become expensive emergencies. Dreads every bill. Feels like the system doesn't work for them.

Success

$0 copays, $0 deductibles. A real relationship with their doctor. Healthcare that works when they need it — without dread.

Transformation: From avoiding care due to cost → Getting the care they need. From dreading the bill → Never worrying about it.
