Site Architecture

How the Vxtra Health website is structured

Architecture Philosophy

The Vxtra Health website operates as an “Air Traffic Control” homepage — a single entry point that quickly identifies each visitor’s role and routes them to a persona-specific experience. Every path leads to the same destination: a scheduled conversation.

The site is built for two simultaneous audiences: 1. Augusta & Gainesville visitors — routed toward local brokers and market-specific messaging 2. Outside-market visitors — routed toward general consultation and interest capture

Strategy

Corporate Positioning Strategy

Brand position, differentiation, and channel approach

1. Brand Position Statement

Vxtra Health is a physician-led health plan for self-insured middle-market employers in secondary markets.

Vxtra is not an insurance company. Vxtra is not a TPA (third-party administrator). Vxtra is not a broker. Vxtra is not a point solution.

Vxtra is the orchestrator — the entity that connects local independent physicians, self-funded employers, Fortune 100 technology (Capital Rx), and stop-loss reinsurance (Tokyo Marine HCC) into a single, community-based health plan that costs less, performs better, and gives everyone at the table more control.

Position in one sentence: > Vxtra Health is a physician-led health plan that brings Fortune 100 healthcare technology to mid-market employers — connecting them with the best local doctors in their community for predictable costs and a better experience for everyone.

What Vxtra replaces: The traditional insurance carrier’s administrative role in a self-funded arrangement. Employers keep their self-funded structure but replace the carrier’s network, claims processing, drug pricing, and data reporting with Vxtra’s integrated platform.

2. Competitive Positioning

vs. Traditional Carriers (Blue Cross, United, Cigna, Aetna)

Dimension Traditional Carrier Vxtra Health
Data speed 120-day delay 30 days or less
Drug pricing Opaque PBM spreads NADAC transparent pricing
Physician relationship Pre-auths, denials, bureaucracy Direct employer-physician, no pre-auths
Incentive alignment Profit from higher costs (1,600% stock growth in 8 years) Revenue from better outcomes and lower costs
Technology Legacy systems built for billing Capital Rx: first platform processing Rx and medical claims together
Care coordination Call center (saves insurer money) Vxtra Care concierge (helps patient navigate care)
Local presence National bureaucracy “Our Town” community model

Phase 1 messaging (through mid-2027): Position as “a better option” and “a way out.” Emphasize what Vxtra offers rather than what carriers do wrong. Pro-employer, pro-transparency framing. Vxtra must operate within the Cigna network in Augusta until proprietary networks are live.

Phase 2 messaging (mid-2027+): Shift to direct contrast. The data and outcomes from Augusta will speak for themselves. Stronger anti-carrier messaging becomes viable once Vxtra is no longer dependent on Cigna’s network.

vs. Captive Insurance Arrangements

Dimension Captive Vxtra Health
Capital requirement Employer puts capital at risk upfront No employer capital required — Tokyo Marine HCC provides stop-loss
Complexity High — requires employer to understand insurance mechanics Low — Vxtra handles orchestration, employer sees outcomes
Risk pooling Employer bears individual risk “Our Town” aggregation across 20-30 employers in a market
Physician network Uses existing carrier networks Partners with local independent physicians (40-70% lower cost)
Data transparency Varies by captive manager Real-time, data in 30 days or less standard

Key differentiator: Vxtra delivers the collective bargaining power of a captive without requiring employers to put up capital or understand insurance plumbing. Tokyo Marine HCC’s $1.5M investment validates the risk model.

vs. PE-Owned Brokers (60% of the market)

Dimension PE-Owned Broker Independent Broker + Vxtra
Incentive Ride to earn-out, minimize disruption Differentiate, grow, win new business
Innovation Low — PE discourages risk High — Vxtra is the innovation

Vxtra Health sounds like a physician-practice leader who became a CEO — experienced, confident, community-minded, clear. Not a Silicon Valley disruptor. Not an insurance company. Not a political activist. A builder who has seen the system from every side and decided to build something better.

Language Rules

ALWAYS use: - “Physician” or “doctor” — never “provider” - “Health insurance” when describing the problem — not “healthcare” - “Our money” (employer speaking to employees) — not “my money” - “Avoidable cost” — not “fraud” or “waste” - “Agency” (for physicians regaining autonomy) - Dollar amounts: “$25 per employee per month” — not “25% savings” - “Self-funded” or “self-insured” — interchangeable, both acceptable - “Our Town” — for the marketplace/aggregation concept - “Fortune 100 technology” — for Capital Rx capabilities - “The best local physicians” — not “only local physicians” - Short sentences. Active voice. One idea per sentence.

NEVER use: - “Provider” — “They’re not providers. They’re physicians.” - “Healthcare is broken” — The problem is how healthcare is paid for and administered. - “It’s Just Better” — Rejected tagline. Not specific enough. - Insurance jargon in customer-facing materials: EOB, TPA, PBM, CPT, NADAC, stop-loss, captive, ASO, SPD - “Narrow network” or anything that implies geographic limitation - Percentages when dollar amounts are available - Sentences longer than 20 words in headlines or CTAs - More than 2 industry terms in a single paragraph (even internally) - “Disrupt” or “disruptive” — Vxtra builds, it does not disrupt - Aggressive, combative, or accusatory tone (especially Phase 1)

Cognitive Load Rule

Every piece of customer-facing content must pass the cognitive load test: Could a busy CEO read this in 30 seconds and understand what Vxtra does? If the answer is no, simplify. Cut the sentence in half. Remove the jargon. Use a concrete example instead of an abstraction.

“The lower the cognitive load, the more people place orders.” — Larry Hightower

The “Grunt Test” (StoryBrand)

Every page, email, social post, and sales sheet must answer three questions within 5 seconds: 1. What does this company do? 2. How will it make my life better? 3. What do I need to do next?

If a caveman couldn’t grunt the answers, rewrite it.

Vxtra Health is a physician-led health plan, not a traditional carrier. Q3: What does “exclusive access” mean for brokers?

In each market, Vxtra Health partners with a limited number of independent broker agencies. This means you are not competing with other brokers selling the same Vxtra plan. Your territory is protected, and employers in your market can only access Vxtra Health through you.

Q4: What is the compensation structure?

Three-year contracts with 40% upfront commissions. This is designed to reward brokers who make the commitment early and bring employers to the table during market launch. The three-year contract also protects your client relationships and provides income predictability.

No. You maintain your advisory relationship with the employer. We provide you with a sales sheet, an employer-facing presentation deck, a qualification checklist, and an FAQ document. Independent brokers are the backbone of secondary markets. They know the employers, they know the community, and they have long-term relationships built on trust. Vxtra Health was built for brokers who want to innovate and grow — not ride out a contract.

Exclusive partnerships in each market are limited. Network recruitment, outreach, and onboarding

Conversion Expectation: Aim for 60-70% conversion from Stage 3 (1-on-1 conversation) to Stage 5 (join network). Physicians who agree to a private conversation with Larry are already pre-qualified by the warm introduction.

C2. Top 5 Physician Influencers per Market

The “Our Town” model requires identifying 5 key physician influencers in each target market. These are not just any physicians — they are the connectors, the respected voices, the physicians other physicians listen to.

Selection Criteria:

Criterion Why It Matters How to Assess
Independent practice Must not be hospital-employed (aligned incentives) Practice ownership records, state medical board data
Community reputation Trusted by peers and patients, known name in town Larry’s network, medical society leadership roles, community involvement
Specialty mix Need balance across primary care and key specialties Market mapping by specialty — primary care anchor + 2-3 specialists
Patient volume Sufficient panel size to contribute meaningful lives Practice size, number of physicians in the group
Willingness to lead Must be willing to be early adopter and vocal advocate 1-on-1 conversation, gauging enthusiasm and leadership temperament
Peer influence Other physicians respect and follow their decisions Medical society roles, referral patterns, informal reputation
Alignment with mission Frustrated with insurance bureaucracy, values autonomy Conversation — every physician has a story, listen for passion

Ideal Top 5 Composition per Market:

  1. Primary Care Anchor (1-2 physicians): Family medicine or internal medicine group, well-established, large patient panel, community pillar. This is the foundation.
  2. Surgical/Procedural Specialist (1): Orthopedics, general surgery, or urology — high-volume specialty where hospital cost differential (40-70%) is most visible.
  3. Diagnostic/Imaging Specialist (1): Radiology, cardiology, or similar — controls referral flow and diagnostic capacity.
  4. Women’s Health / Pediatrics (1): OB/GYN or pediatrician — critical for employee families, high emotional engagement.

Primary Channels:

  • Larry asks trusted colleagues for introductions to physicians he does not know
  • Every new market begins with Larry mapping who he knows and who he needs to meet

  • Attend events as a peer, not as a vendor
  • Larry’s credibility (physician practice builder, not insurance executive) opens doors
  • Target events where independent physicians congregate — not hospital-affiliated functions
  • Sponsor or speak at events focused on practice management, autonomy, or healthcare economics

  • Physicians trust other physicians over any marketing material
  • Facilitate “physician to physician” conversations — let existing network members tell their own story
  • Create informal networking opportunities (dinners, roundtables) where prospects meet current members

  • Positions the introduction as employer-driven, not sales-driven
  • Particularly effective for physicians who already serve employees of the broker’s clients

C6. Physician Retention Strategy

Recruiting physicians is only half the challenge. Retaining them — and turning them into advocates — requires sustained engagement and visible results.

1. Community of Practice Create a peer network among Vxtra physicians. Physicians who feel connected to other physicians in the network are significantly less likely to leave.

  • Quarterly physician dinners in each market (informal, peer-to-peer, no sales pitch)
  • Private online forum or group (LinkedIn group or dedicated platform) for Vxtra physicians to share experiences, ask questions, and connect
  • Annual Vxtra Physician Summit (once network reaches 3+ markets) — thought leadership, practice management, networking

2. Data-Driven Outcomes Reporting Physicians are scientists. Show them the data.

  • Monthly practice dashboard: patient volume, claims processed, average resolution time
  • Quarterly outcomes report: patient satisfaction scores, treatment completion rates, preventive care metrics
  • Annual benchmarking: compare Vxtra network outcomes to regional and national averages
  • Transparency: physicians see the same data employers see — no hidden metrics

3. Quarterly Physician Meetings Structured meetings in each market, every quarter.

  • Review network performance data
  • Discuss patient care patterns and opportunities
  • Address any operational issues or improvement suggestions
  • Introduce new employers and projected patient panel growth
  • Guest speaker or educational component (CME-eligible when possible)

4. Peer Referral Recognition Physicians who refer other physicians to the network are acknowledged.

  • Personal thank-you from Larry
  • Recognition at quarterly meetings
  • Priority consideration for new patient panel assignments
  • No financial incentive (inappropriate for physician relationships) — recognition and respect are the currency

5. Administrative Burden Monitoring Track and actively reduce the administrative burden on Vxtra physicians compared to their traditional insurance experience.

  • Quarterly survey: “How does working with Vxtra compare to working with [traditional insurer]?”
  • Track metrics: time from treatment decision to treatment delivery, claims processing speed, number of administrative interactions required per patient
  • Set improvement targets and report progress to physicians

6. Practice Growth Support Help Vxtra physicians grow their practices through the network.

  • As new employers join, expand physician patient panels
  • Provide market data on community healthcare needs and gaps
  • Facilitate connections between primary care physicians and specialists within the network
  • Support practice marketing to Vxtra employer employees (co-branded materials, directory listing)

C7. Key Messaging by Specialty

Not all physicians have the same pain points. While the core message (autonomy, no pre-auths, independent practice) resonates universally, the emphasis should shift based on specialty.

Primary Care (Family Medicine / Internal Medicine) — FIRST PRIORITY

Primary pain point: Volume pressure and administrative burden. Primary care physicians spend the most time on pre-authorizations because they manage the broadest range of conditions and referrals.

Key messages: - “Spend more time with each patient — not on paperwork” - “Your referrals go directly to trusted local specialists — no insurance gatekeeper” - “Build a real panel of patients from local employers who stay with you long-term” - “Vxtra patients are not cycling through on 36-month insurance rotations — they are your community”

Pitch emphasis: Patient relationships, reduced administrative burden, referral autonomy, stable patient panel from local employers.

Orthopedics / Musculoskeletal — HIGH VALUE

Primary pain point: Pre-authorizations for imaging (MRI, CT) and surgical procedures. Step therapy protocols that force non-surgical treatment before approving what the physician already knows is necessary.

Key messages: - “You determine when a patient needs an MRI — not an insurance reviewer” - “Independent orthopedic practice costs 40-70% less than hospital-based — Vxtra proves that to employers” - “No step therapy requirements dictating your treatment sequence”

Pitch emphasis: Immediate clinical authority, cost advantage of independent practice, direct relationship with employer (who sees the cost savings).

Cardiology — HIGH VALUE

Primary pain point: Diagnostic pre-authorizations (stress tests, echocardiograms, catheterizations) and medication pre-authorizations (newer cardiac medications frequently denied or delayed).

Key messages: - “Order the diagnostic workup your patient needs — today” - “No prior authorization for cardiac imaging or stress testing” - “Transparent data means you see what your patients need and act immediately”

Pitch emphasis: Speed of diagnostic decision-making, no delays in treatment, technology advantage (transparent data).

OB/GYN — ESSENTIAL FOR FAMILIES

Primary pain point: Maternity care bundling and billing complexity, pre-authorization for procedures, and loss of continuity when patients change insurance plans mid-pregnancy.

  • Incentive: Recognition, small gift cards, or wellness credits

C8. Retention Focus

The benchmark: Traditional insurance companies retain members for 36-42 months on average. Vxtra’s goal is to significantly exceed this through relationship-based care and the Vxtra Care concierge — building loyalty that commodity insurance cannot match.

Why Vxtra Should Retain Longer

Traditional insurance is transactional. Employees have no relationship with their insurer. When the employer switches plans, employees feel nothing — because there was nothing to feel.

Vxtra is relational. The employee has: - A physician who knows them by name - A nurse navigator who has helped them schedule appointments and coordinate care - A history of $0 bills and no runaround - Trust built through repeated positive experiences

Switching away from Vxtra means losing those relationships. That is a powerful retention mechanism — not because of lock-in, but because of genuine value.

Retention Strategy: Employee Side

  1. Deepen the physician relationship — Encourage annual wellness visits, proactive check-ins, and continuity of care. The longer the doctor-patient relationship, the harder it is to walk away.

  2. Vxtra Care as the stickiness layer — Every interaction with Vxtra Care builds switching cost. Not financial switching cost — emotional switching cost. “Who is going to schedule my specialist visits if I lose Vxtra Care?”

  3. Celebrate milestones — One-year anniversary email: “You have been with Vxtra for a year. Here is what we did together: 4 doctor visits, 2 specialist referrals coordinated by Vxtra Care, $0 out of pocket.” Make the value tangible and personal.

  4. Collect and share stories — Employee testimonials (with permission) create social proof. “I never used to go to the doctor. Now I actually look forward to my check-ups.” These stories reinforce the community feeling and become tools for employer retention conversations.

  5. Anticipate renewal season — 90 days before the employer’s renewal decision, proactively reach out to employees with a satisfaction survey. Share aggregate results with the employer. Let employee sentiment be the most powerful renewal argument.

Retention Strategy: Employer Side (Employee-Driven)

The strongest argument for employer renewal is employee satisfaction. Structure reporting to make this undeniable:

  • “What your employees are saying” — Include anonymized employee quotes in every quarterly review
  • Utilization proof — “87% of your employees have seen their physician. 62% have used Vxtra Care. Your previous plan had 41% utilization.”
  • Cost avoidance narrative — “8% of people spend 80% of healthcare dollars. Vxtra Care helped [X] employees get preventive care that caught issues early — before they became expensive emergencies.”
  • Retention correlation — Track whether employees with higher Vxtra engagement have lower turnover. Even anecdotal evidence is compelling.

The Long Game

Vxtra’s retention advantage is not a gimmick or a contract clause. It is the natural result of a system that actually works for people. When someone has a doctor who knows them, a care team that handles the hard parts, and $0 bills — they do not want to go back to the old way.

The employee becomes Vxtra’s most powerful advocate at renewal time. Not because anyone asked them to. Because they mean it.

Fractional CMO Recommendation

We recommend a fractional CMO / marketing partnership model over a full-time marketing hire for the first 12-18 months. Here is why:

  1. Cost efficiency. A full-time VP of Marketing or CMO costs $150K-$250K+ in salary and benefits. A fractional engagement delivers senior strategic leadership at a fraction of that cost — critical when working capital is $2M and every dollar needs to drive toward 500 enrolled employees.

  2. Immediate capability. There is no 3-month ramp-up period. Business Builders already has full context from the StoryBrand workshop, understands the messaging, knows the personas, and has the production team ready to execute. Day one is productive.

  3. Full team, not one person. A fractional model through Business Builders provides a strategist, designer, developer, copywriter, and video coordinator — not a single marketing generalist trying to do everything alone.

  4. Flexibility. As Vxtra grows and funding scales (private placement $3.5M in Q3, additional markets in 2027), the engagement can scale up or transition to an in-house hire supported by the agency. The fractional model bridges the gap.

  5. Sandra’s bandwidth. Larry acknowledged that Sandra is running full-time operations and no one on the Vxtra team is dedicated to marketing. A fractional partner handles execution so the Vxtra team can focus on what they do best — building the health plan and serving employers.

The right time to evaluate a full-time marketing hire is after Augusta has proven the model (Q1 2027) and Gainesville launch planning demands dedicated in-house coordination.
