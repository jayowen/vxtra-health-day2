### 2. PROBLEM SECTION (PEACE Framework Staircase)

**Section Label:** THE PROBLEM

**P — Problem (Attention Grabber)**

**Headline:** It's hard to differentiate yourself in the marketplace. Everyone is selling the same thing.

**Body:** You sit across from an employer and present the same Blue Cross, United, or Cigna plans that three other brokers already pitched this month. The carriers are the same. The networks are the same. The renewal increases are the same. And the employer knows it.

---

**E — Empathy (Build the Bond)**

**Headline:** It's exhausting trying to stand out with the same solutions as every other broker in town.

**Body:** You've built your business on relationships and trust. But relationships only take you so far when another broker can walk in with the same exact product at the same exact price. Meanwhile, 60% of brokerages are now owned by private equity firms — and they're coming for your clients. Not because they're better. Because they're bigger.

---

**A — Answer (Position the Solution)**

**Headline:** What if you had a health plan your competitors couldn't access?

**Body:** Vxtra Health gives you a transparent, physician-led health plan backed by the same technology Fortune 100 companies use — and your competitors don't have access to it. Transparent data instead of waiting 120 days for reports. Local physicians instead of faceless networks. Lower costs that you can prove with actual numbers, not promises. This is the plan that makes employers call you back.

---

**C — Change (Aspirational Identity)**

**Headline:** Be the broker who brings something nobody else can offer.

**Body:** When you partner with Vxtra Health, you stop being one of five brokers pitching the same renewal. You become the only broker in your market with access to a plan that delivers Fortune 100 benefits to mid-market employers. Existing clients stay because they can't get this anywhere else. New employers come to you because word travels fast in a secondary market.

---

**E — End Result (Brand Promise)**

**Headline:** Your clients save real money. Your book of business is protected. And employers come to you.

**Body:** Your clients get lower, predictable costs with transparent data they can actually see and control. Their employees get $0 copays, $0 deductibles, and personal care navigation from real physicians — not a call center. And you? You have a product no competitor can match, your existing business is secured, and your pipeline is full.

### 6. FAILURE / STAKES

**Section Label:** THE STAKES

**Section Headline:** What happens if you keep selling the same plans as everyone else.

---

**Failure Point 1: Another Agency Shares Vxtra First**

Vxtra Health is not a product that will be available to every broker in every market. Access is exclusive and limited. If another independent agency in your market partners with Vxtra Health before you do, they have the differentiation — and you are back to competing on relationships alone. In a market where 60% of brokerages are PE-owned and growing, that is not a position you want to be in.

---

**Failure Point 2: Clients Leave for Someone with a Better Story**

Every renewal season, your clients get calls from other brokers. Most of those calls end in "we have the same carriers you already have." But if one of those brokers has something genuinely different — lower costs, better data, a physician-led model — that call ends differently. Client retention is not about relationships alone. It is about having something worth staying for.

---

**Failure Point 3: Revenue Stagnates While the Market Changes**

The health insurance market is consolidating. Private equity firms are buying independent agencies. Insurance carriers are raising rates. And employers are increasingly frustrated. The brokers who grow through the next decade will be the ones who brought something new to the table. The ones who kept selling the same thing? They are the ones getting acquired — or replaced.

### 10. FAQ SECTION

**Section Label:** QUESTIONS FROM BROKERS

**Section Headline:** Answers to what we hear most.

---

**Q1: What kind of employers qualify for Vxtra Health?**

Self-insured employers with 100 to 2,000 employees who have been self-funded for at least two years. We collect basic census data (no personal information needed) to determine fit. Your existing self-funded clients are a natural starting point.

---

**Q2: How is Vxtra Health different from the carriers I already sell?**

Vxtra Health is a physician-led health plan, not a traditional carrier. We partner with local independent physicians, use Fortune 100 technology for transparent data and drug pricing, and provide a care orchestration service for employees. The result: lower costs for employers, a better experience for employees, and transparent data you can present to a board. Traditional carriers cannot replicate this model because their economics are built differently.

---

**Q3: What does "exclusive access" mean for brokers?**

In each market, Vxtra Health partners with a limited number of independent broker agencies. This means you are not competing with other brokers selling the same Vxtra plan. Your territory is protected, and employers in your market can only access Vxtra Health through you.

---

**Q4: What is the compensation structure?**

Three-year contracts with 40% upfront commissions. This is designed to reward brokers who make the commitment early and bring employers to the table during market launch. The three-year contract also protects your client relationships and provides income predictability.

---

**Q5: Do I need to learn a completely new system or process?**

No. You maintain your advisory relationship with the employer. We provide you with a sales sheet, an employer-facing presentation deck, a qualification checklist, and an FAQ document. We also support you through the employer conversation — you do not need to become a health plan expert overnight. We handle the plan design, technology, and physician network. You bring the relationships.

---

**Q6: Why independent brokers specifically? Why not PE-owned firms?**

Independent brokers are the backbone of secondary markets. They know the employers, they know the community, and they have long-term relationships built on trust. PE-owned firms are typically optimizing for earn-out timelines and will not invest in a new model that disrupts the status quo. Vxtra Health was built for brokers who want to innovate and grow — not ride out a contract.

---

**Q7: What happens if I wait?**

Exclusive partnerships in each market are limited. If another independent agency in your market partners with Vxtra Health before you, they have the exclusive access — and the differentiation that comes with it. We are actively having conversations in Augusta and Gainesville now, with additional markets on the roadmap for 2027 and beyond.

### 7. EXCLUSIVITY / TERRITORY MODEL

---

**How Exclusive Access Works**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Unit of Territory | One secondary market (defined by metro area / MSA) |
| Brokers per Market | 2-4 independent agencies per market (varies by market size) |
| Contract Length | 3 years |
| Exclusivity Scope | Only partner agencies can offer Vxtra Health to employers in that market |
| Overlap Protection | No PE-owned firms. No national wirehouses. Independent agencies only. |
| Performance Expectation | Minimum employer introductions per quarter (to be defined per market) |

---

**Territory Protection**

- Once a broker partnership is signed for a market, no additional broker agencies are added to that market beyond the agreed limit.
- Brokers are encouraged to bring multiple employers simultaneously (Kelly's model in Augusta) rather than one at a time.
- If a broker consistently fails to meet minimum activity requirements (no employer introductions for 6+ months), Vxtra reserves the right to open additional partnerships in that market — but only after a formal conversation and remediation period.

---

**Early-Mover Advantage**

| Advantage | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| First access to the product | Brokers who partner during market launch have the longest runway and deepest client relationships |
| Lowest competition | Fewer partner agencies in early markets — more territory per broker |
| Peer credibility | "I was one of the first broker partners" carries weight in a secondary market |
| Commission priority | Launch-phase brokers receive preferred commission structures (40% upfront) |
| Input into the model | Early partners provide feedback that shapes how Vxtra works with brokers long-term |

---

**Messaging for Exclusivity:**
- "Exclusive partnerships are limited to [X] independent agencies per market."
- "If another agency in your market partners with Vxtra Health before you, they have the exclusive access."
- "This is not a product that will be available to every broker in every market."
- "The brokers who move first get the deepest relationships and the most protected territory."
