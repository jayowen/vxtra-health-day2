Page-Level Notes

  • StoryBrand Alignment: The page follows the full 7-part StoryBrand framework in order: Character (hero), Problem (villain), Guide (empathy + authority), Plan (3 steps), Success (outcome), Failure (stakes), CTA (call to action).
  • PEACE Framework Integration: The employer PEACE #1 (cost-focused) is embedded across the page: P = hero headline, E = problem section title, A = comparison table + Our Town model, C = success card subtitles, E = success card body copy.

SECTION B: Employer Targeted Marketing Strategy

Converting self-insured employers (200-2,000 employees) in Augusta, GA (July 2026) and Gainesville, GA (July 2027)

1. Conversion Funnel

The employer conversion funnel operates through four stages. The broker is the primary facilitator at every stage – Vxtra does not go direct to employers without broker involvement.

Stage 1: AWARENESS

“I didn’t know there was another option.”

Element Detail
Goal Make self-insured employers in Augusta and Gainesville aware that an alternative to traditional health insurance administration exists
Channels Larry’s LinkedIn thought leadership, broker conversations, civic group presentations (Rotary, Chamber of Commerce), remarketing ads, local business press
Content Problem-focused: “Health insurance costs are crushing your business” messaging, industry stats (7-10% annual increases, 1,600% insurance stock appreciation), FAQ videos
Key Touchpoints Larry speaks at Augusta Rotary/Chamber; Kelly and Salem mention Vxtra in existing client conversations; LinkedIn posts reach healthcare executives; website remarketing tags visitors
Conversion Event Visits vxtrahealth.com OR broker introduces Vxtra in a client meeting

Stage 2: INTEREST

“Tell me more about how this works.”

Element Detail
Goal Move from awareness to active exploration. Employer begins researching and asking questions.
Channels Website (index-v2.html), lead magnets (PDF guide, cost calculator), broker-facilitated introductions, email nurture sequence
Content Solution-focused: How the Our Town model works, comparison table (traditional vs. Vxtra), Capital Rx technology story, Vxtra Care concierge overview
Key Touchpoints Employer downloads “Smart CEO’s Roadmap” PDF; employer uses cost calculator tool; broker schedules a three-way call with Vxtra; employer watches FAQ video series
Conversion Event Requests a free cost analysis OR schedules a discovery conversation

Stage 3: EVALUATION

“Is this real? Will it work for my company?”

Element Detail
Goal Qualify the employer and demonstrate Vxtra’s specific value for their workforce
Channels One-on-one discovery calls (Larry/Sandra + broker), custom plan design presentation, peer-to-peer conversations with other employers considering Vxtra
Content Proof-focused: Custom ROI projection using employer’s census data, Capital Rx case studies, Tokyo Marine HCC backing, Larry’s track record (22x and 15x exits, urology practice growth)
Key Touchpoints Discovery call with Larry/Sandra; census data submitted for analysis; custom plan design delivered; employer talks to another employer who is also evaluating (the “dancing guy” effect)
Conversion Event Employer receives custom plan design and ROI projection

Stage 4: DECISION

“I’m ready to move forward.”

Element Detail
Goal Secure letter of intent and begin onboarding
Channels Broker-led close, Larry/Sandra support, legal review
Content Commitment-focused: Letter of intent template, onboarding timeline, implementation roadmap, employee communication plan
Key Touchpoints Broker presents final recommendation; employer signs letter of intent; onboarding kickoff scheduled
Conversion Event Signed letter of intent

2. Lead Magnets / Transitional CTAs

Each lead magnet serves a specific stage of the funnel and captures contact information for the email nurture sequence.

Lead Magnet 1: “The Smart CEO’s Roadmap to Better Health Benefits”

  • Format: PDF guide, 8-12 pages
  • Stage: Awareness to Interest
  • Content: The problem with traditional health insurance (stats-driven), what self-funded employers should know, 5 questions to ask your broker, what “Fortune 100 technology” means for mid-market companies, the Our Town model overview
  • CTA placement: Website hero secondary button, LinkedIn posts, email signatures, broker handouts
  • Capture: Name, email, company name, company size
  • Follow-up: Enters email nurture sequence

Lead Magnet 2: Free Cost Analysis (Census Data Review)

  • Format: Personalized analysis delivered via call
  • Stage: Interest to Evaluation
  • Content: Employer submits anonymized census data (number of employees, current plan type, approximate spend). Vxtra team runs analysis showing projected savings with the Vxtra model.
  • CTA placement: Website primary CTA, broker conversations, email sequence
  • Capture: Full contact info + census data (no personal employee details needed)
  • Follow-up: Scheduled discovery call with Larry/Sandra + broker
  • Qualification gate: Minimum 100+ employees on plan, self-insured 2+ years

Lead Magnet 3: Cost Compounding Calculator

  • Format: Interactive web tool
  • Stage: Awareness to Interest
  • Content: Employer inputs current annual health insurance spend and number of employees. Calculator shows 5-year and 10-year cost projection at 7%, 8%, and 10% annual increases. Side-by-side comparison with Vxtra’s projected savings. Output: “At your current rate, you’ll spend $X over the next decade. With Vxtra, that number drops to $Y.”
  • CTA placement: Website, LinkedIn posts, email sequence, broker presentations
  • Capture: Email required to see results
  • Follow-up: Enters email nurture sequence with personalized follow-up

Lead Magnet 4: ROI Projection Tool

  • Format: Custom PDF report generated from census data
  • Stage: Evaluation
  • Content: Detailed breakdown: current per-employee-per-month cost vs. projected Vxtra cost, savings by category (Rx, specialist visits, ER avoidance), total annual savings estimate, 3-year projection
  • CTA placement: Delivered during evaluation stage after census data collection
  • Capture: Already qualified by this point
  • Follow-up: Scheduled presentation call

Lead Magnet 5: Webinar – “What Self-Funded Employers Need to Know in 2026”

  • Format: 45-minute live webinar (recorded for on-demand replay)
  • Stage: Awareness to Interest
  • Content: Larry presents the state of self-funded health insurance, the problem with legacy carriers, what Fortune 100 companies are doing differently (Capital Rx angle), how secondary markets can access the same tools, Q&A
  • CTA placement: LinkedIn promotion, email list, broker invitations, Chamber/Rotary follow-up
  • Capture: Name, email, company name, role, company size
  • Follow-up: Recording sent + enters email nurture sequence
  • Timing: First webinar April 2026 (pre-launch), quarterly thereafter

3. Email Nurture Sequence

Triggered after any lead magnet capture. 7-email sequence over 21 days. All emails come from Larry Hightower (personal, not brand@).

Email 1: Welcome + The Problem (Day 0)

  • Subject Line: “The math on health insurance isn’t getting friendlier”
  • Key Message: Thank them for downloading/registering. Restate the core problem: costs are rising 7-10% annually, doubling in a decade. Acknowledge the frustration – “We know renewal season feels like a gut punch.” Brief intro to Vxtra and the Our Town model.
  • CTA: “Reply to this email with your biggest health insurance frustration”
  • Tone: Empathetic, personal, conversational

Email 2: The Villain (Day 3)

  • Subject Line: “Where your health insurance premiums actually go”
  • Key Message: The 1,600% insurance stock appreciation stat. The fact that insurance companies are administrators for self-funded plans, not risk-bearers – yet they still extract massive fees. Hospital administrators grew 2,500% vs. 100% physician growth. Frame the systemic misalignment without naming specific insurers (Cigna constraint).
  • CTA: Link to FAQ video: “Why do health insurance costs keep going up?”
  • Tone: Educational, factual, controlled frustration

Email 3: The Technology Story (Day 6)

  • Subject Line: “Your company deserves Fortune 100 benefits”
  • Key Message: Introduce Capital Rx and the Judi platform. Transparent data in 30 days or less (vs. 120-day industry standard). First platform to process medical and Rx claims together. NADAC drug pricing. “Until now, this technology was only available to the largest companies in the world. Vxtra Health brings it to employers with 200 to 2,000 employees.”
  • CTA: Link to cost calculator tool
  • Tone: Optimistic, forward-looking, credibility-building

Email 4: The Our Town Model (Day 9)

  • Subject Line: “In Augusta, the dots are closer together”
  • Key Message: Explain how the Our Town model works in plain language. Local independent physicians (same treatment, 40-70% less than hospital systems). Collective purchasing power when employers pool together. “People like us do things like this” community messaging. Name Augusta specifically.
  • CTA: “See how employers in Augusta are coming together” (link to Our Town section of website)
  • Tone: Community-driven, warm, local pride

Email 5: The Employee Experience (Day 13)

  • Subject Line: “What if your employees actually loved their health plan?”

  • CTA: “Schedule a conversation about what this looks like for your team”
  • Tone: Aspirational, employee-centric, vision-casting

Email 6: Social Proof + Urgency (Day 17)

  • Subject Line: “Augusta employers are already asking about this”

  • Key Message: The direct ask. “I’d like to schedule a 30-minute conversation with you and your broker. No pitch, no pressure – just an honest look at whether Vxtra is the right fit for your company.” Restate the transformation: “From feeling trapped every renewal cycle to having a plan your employees love and costs you can actually control.”

Sequence Notes:

  • If recipient clicks “Schedule a Call” at any point, remove from nurture and move to qualified pipeline
  • If recipient downloads the cost analysis lead magnet, skip to Email 5 (they’ve already engaged with the problem/solution)
  • After Email 7, move to monthly newsletter list (not dead – just not ready yet)
  • All emails should be plain-text style (no heavy HTML templates) – feels like a real email from Larry

4. Broker-Facilitated Conversion Strategy

Brokers are the gateway to employers. Vxtra never goes direct. The broker relationship is protected and compensated (3-year contracts, 40% upfront commissions).

Augusta Market: Kelly and Salem

Kelly and Salem have completed 1.5 years of groundwork in Augusta. They are at 8/10 readiness. Their approach is unique and critical to the go-to-market strategy:

Multi-Employer Table Strategy Kelly’s approach is to bring multiple employers to the table at once – not one-by-one selling. This is foundational to the Our Town model because: - Employers see peer validation immediately (“I’m not the only one considering this”) - Collective volume triggers better stop-loss pricing from Jay Richie / Tokyo Marine HCC - Accelerates the path to 5,000 covered lives threshold - Reduces individual employer risk perception (“If Company X is in, this must be real”)

The “Dancing Guy” Approach The first employer to sign is the leader. The second employer validates. Then momentum builds naturally. Kelly and Salem facilitate this through: - Golf course introductions between business owners - Owner-to-owner peer conversations (“Let me tell you what I’m looking at for our health plan”) - Informal selling that’s already happening: “I already told three people about this” - Civic group overlap (Rotary, Chamber members who are also employer decision-makers)

Broker Enablement Materials (What Kelly and Salem Need) 1. Single-page sales sheet – outcome-focused, not operational detail (“don’t tell them how to build the watch”) 2. Qualification checklist – 100+ employees on plan, self-insured 2+ years, census data process 3. Objection response guide – top 10 employer objections with StoryBrand-aligned responses 4. Employer introduction email template – broker sends to client with Vxtra overview 5. Discovery call scheduling link – broker books the three-way with Vxtra directly

Strategy Summary

The employer marketing strategy for Vxtra Health is fundamentally a broker-facilitated, community-driven, education-first approach. It is not a traditional digital marketing play. The strategy works because:

  1. Brokers are the channel. Kelly/Salem in Augusta and Bart/Sam in Gainesville are the trusted advisors who open doors. Marketing supports them – it does not replace them.

  2. The community IS the market. In Augusta and Gainesville, “the dots are closer together.” Civic groups, peer-to-peer conversations, and golf course introductions matter more than ad spend.

  3. Education builds trust. Per Larry’s principle: “educate without offending.” The FAQ videos, blog content, and civic presentations all follow the Marcus Sheridan model – answer real questions honestly.

  4. Larry is the brand. His personal credibility (40+ years, multiple exits, physician practice growth) is the foundation of all authority. His LinkedIn presence, speaking engagements, and one-on-one conversations are the highest-leverage marketing activities.

  5. Capital Rx is the proof. Riding the “wake” of Capital Rx’s Fortune 100 announcements (Apple, GM) validates the technology story without Vxtra having to prove it independently.

  6. Urgency is organic. The July 2026 launch window, the collective pool forming, and the “dancing guy” momentum create natural urgency – no artificial scarcity needed.

The goal is not leads. The goal is 10 letters of intent in Augusta by June 30, 2026, representing 5,000+ covered lives. Everything in this strategy is built to achieve that single outcome.
