SECTION A: Physician Landing Page Copy

Conversion-focused copy for a dedicated physician landing page. Follows StoryBrand structure. Tone: respectful, empowering, never pitying. Always “physician” — never “provider.” Core theme: independence and clinical autonomy.

A1. Hero Section

Label Badge: Now Accepting Physicians in Augusta, GA

Headline: You became a physician to practice medicine — not to fight insurance companies.

Subheadline: Vxtra Health is building a physician-led network where you make the clinical decisions, work directly with local employers, and run an independent practice in a community that knows your name. No pre-authorizations. No denials. Just medicine.

Primary CTA: Join the Network Secondary CTA: Learn How It Works

A2. Problem Section (PEACE Staircase)

Section Label: THE REAL PROBLEM

Lead-In: Insurance companies are practicing medicine for you.

Problem Statement 1 — Lost Authority: You diagnose the patient. You determine the treatment plan. Then an insurance company employee who has never examined your patient overrides your clinical judgment with a denial letter. Every day, physicians across the country lose hours to pre-authorization forms, peer-to-peer reviews, and appeals — time that should be spent with patients.

Problem Statement 2 — The Bureaucracy Tax: The average physician now spends more time on paperwork than patient care. Pre-authorizations delay treatment. Denials force re-submissions. Step therapy protocols override clinical expertise. The system was designed to reduce insurance company costs — not to improve patient outcomes.

Problem Statement 3 — Vanishing Independence: Hospital systems are buying up independent practices at an unprecedented rate, promising relief from the administrative burden but delivering even less autonomy. Once you’re employed by a hospital, your patient volume targets, referral patterns, and even your treatment decisions are dictated by administrators — not by you. And the same treatment that costs 40-70% less in your independent practice now carries hospital-system pricing that your patients can barely afford.

Empathy Close: You didn’t spend a decade in training to fight pre-authorization forms and get told how to treat your own patients by someone who has never seen them.

A3. Value Stack

Section Label: WHAT CHANGES WITH VXTRA HEALTH

Headline: A network designed by physicians, for physicians.

Subheadline: Vxtra Health removes the insurance company from the exam room and gives you back what you earned.

Value 1 — No Pre-Authorizations Headline: Your clinical decisions are final. Body: When you determine a patient needs treatment, that treatment happens. No pre-authorization forms. No peer-to-peer reviews with someone reading from a script. No delays. You order it. It gets done.

Value 2 — No Denials Headline: Your patients get the care you prescribe. Body: The insurance denial machine does not exist in the Vxtra network. When you make a clinical decision, it stands. Your expertise is the authority — not an algorithm built to minimize payouts.

Value 3 — Direct Relationship with Local Employers Headline: You work with the community, not a faceless corporation. Body: Vxtra connects you directly with self-insured employers in your market. These are local companies whose employees live in your community. They want their people to see physicians they trust — and they want those physicians to have full clinical authority.

Value 4 — Technology Handles the Administration Headline: Fortune 100 technology. Zero administrative burden on you. Body: Vxtra’s technology platform — powered by Capital Rx — processes claims, manages data, and handles the administrative work that currently consumes your day. You get transparent data on your patient panel without the paperwork. Claims process in 30 days or less, not 120.

Value 5 — Independent Practice, Protected Headline: Stay independent. Stay in charge. Body: Vxtra was built to strengthen independent physician practices — not to acquire them. You maintain your practice, your staff, your patient relationships, and your clinical autonomy. Vxtra brings you patients, handles the business side, and stays out of your exam room.

CTA: Join the Network

A6. Stakes Section

Section Label: WHAT HAPPENS IF NOTHING CHANGES

Headline: The status quo is not standing still. It is accelerating.

Stake 1 — More Pre-Authorizations, Less Autonomy Insurance companies add new pre-authorization requirements every year. The administrative burden is growing, not shrinking. Every new requirement is another decision taken out of your hands.

Stake 2 — Hospital Consolidation Is Accelerating Hospital systems are acquiring independent practices at record pace, offering buyouts that come with employment contracts — and employment contracts come with productivity quotas, referral mandates, and corporate oversight of your clinical decisions.

Stake 3 — The Numbers Tell the Story 80% of hospital-employed physicians report wanting to return to independent practice. Physician pay has declined 33% since 2001 when adjusted for inflation. Hospital administrators grew 2,500% between 1999 and 2014, while physician growth was approximately 100%. The system is being built around administrators, not around you.

Stake 4 — Your Patients Feel It Too When you lose clinical authority, your patients lose their trusted physician. They get routed through call centers, wait for pre-authorization approvals, and receive care dictated by cost models — not by the person who examined them.

A7. Success Vision

Section Label: IMAGINE THIS INSTEAD

Headline: Practice medicine the way you always intended to.

Vision 1 — Agency Restored You walk into the exam room and your clinical judgment is the final word. No pre-authorization queue. No denial letter waiting in your inbox. Your patient needs treatment — they get it. Today.

Vision 2 — Community-Centered Care Your patients are your neighbors. They work at local companies. They see you at the grocery store, at their kids’ school events, at community gatherings. This is medicine the way it was meant to work — physicians and patients in the same community, building real relationships over years.

Vision 3 — Independent Practice, Sustainable You run your own practice. You set your own standards. Your overhead is manageable because you are not carrying hospital-system cost structures. You are part of a network that supports your independence rather than threatening it.

Vision 4 — Your Patients Trust YOU Not a call center. Not an insurance company website. Not a prior authorization department. Your patients call your office, they see you, and they trust that the care plan you designed is the care plan they will receive.

Closing Line: Your patients trust you — not a call center. You make the clinical decisions. And you run an independent practice in a community that knows your name.

A10. FAQ Section

Section Label: QUESTIONS PHYSICIANS ASK

Q1: How does payment work? A: Vxtra Health works with self-insured employers who fund their own health plans. You are paid directly for the care you deliver — on time, at agreed-upon rates, without the claim denials and delayed reimbursements that come from traditional insurance companies. Claims are processed through our technology platform in 30 days or less, compared to the 120-day cycle typical with legacy insurers.

Q2: What is the time commitment to participate? A: Joining the Vxtra network does not require exclusive participation. You continue seeing your existing patients from all sources. Vxtra adds a new patient stream from local employers — employees who live and work in your community. The administrative burden is minimal because our technology handles claims, data, and coordination.

Q3: Do I keep my other patients and insurance contracts? A: Yes. Vxtra is additive, not exclusive. You maintain your existing patient panel and all current contracts. Vxtra brings additional patients to your practice from local self-insured employers. Over time, many physicians find that the Vxtra patient panel grows naturally as employers in the community join the network.

Q4: What technology do I need to set up? A: Our technology platform integrates with your existing practice management system. There is no major system overhaul required. The Capital Rx platform handles claims processing and data — you interact with a straightforward dashboard that shows your patient panel, claims status, and outcomes data in real time.

Q5: Who makes the clinical decisions? A: You do. That is the foundational principle of the Vxtra network. There are no pre-authorizations. There are no insurance company denials of your treatment plans. You examine the patient, determine the treatment, and proceed. Vxtra’s care orchestration team supports coordination (scheduling, referrals, follow-up) — but clinical authority stays with you.

Q6: Is this only for primary care physicians? A: Vxtra is building a comprehensive local physician network. We start with primary care because that is where the patient relationship begins, but we also need specialists. As the network grows in your market, Vxtra adds specialty coverage so that patients can receive coordinated care within the community. If you are a specialist, we want to hear from you.

The physician recruitment funnel is relationship-driven by design. Physicians do not respond to cold outreach from health plan companies. They respond to peer recommendations and trusted relationships.

Identify independent physicians in target market
Focus: primary care first, then key specialties

C5. Physician Onboarding Experience

Once a physician joins the network, the onboarding process should feel effortless. The entire value proposition is reducing administrative burden — onboarding must reflect that promise from Day 1.

Onboarding Timeline: 4 Weeks

Week 1 — Welcome & Technology Setup - Welcome call from Larry or Sandra (personal, not automated) - Assign dedicated onboarding contact from Vxtra operations team - Technology assessment: review existing practice management system - Capital Rx platform integration planning (compatibility check, data migration scope) - Provide login credentials and dashboard walkthrough

Week 2 — Platform Training & Configuration - 60-minute training session for physician and office staff - Dashboard overview: patient panel view, claims status, outcomes data - Practice management system integration goes live - Test claims submission and verify 30-day-or-less processing cycle - Configure patient routing preferences and scheduling

Week 3 — Patient Panel Assignment - Employer patient lists loaded into system - Physician receives patient panel overview (demographics, basic history if available) - Care orchestration team introduction — meet the people who handle scheduling, referrals, follow-up - Verify referral pathways to other network physicians (specialists) - First patients can begin scheduling

Week 4 — Go-Live & Support - First Vxtra patients seen - Daily check-in with onboarding contact for first week of live patients - Rapid response support line for any claims, technology, or coordination issues - Feedback session at end of Week 4: what is working, what needs adjustment - Transition from onboarding contact to ongoing practice support team

Ongoing Support: - Dedicated practice support contact (not a call center) - Quarterly data review: patient outcomes, panel growth, claims data - Technology updates and platform improvements communicated proactively - Care orchestration team available for complex case coordination

C6. Physician Retention Strategy

Recruiting physicians is only half the challenge. Retaining them — and turning them into advocates — requires sustained engagement and visible results.

1. Community of Practice Create a peer network among Vxtra physicians. Physicians who feel connected to other physicians in the network are significantly less likely to leave.

  • Quarterly physician dinners in each market (informal, peer-to-peer, no sales pitch)
  • Private online forum or group (LinkedIn group or dedicated platform) for Vxtra physicians to share experiences, ask questions, and connect
  • Annual Vxtra Physician Summit (once network reaches 3+ markets) — thought leadership, practice management, networking

2. Data-Driven Outcomes Reporting Physicians are scientists. Show them the data.

  • Monthly practice dashboard: patient volume, claims processed, average resolution time
  • Quarterly outcomes report: patient satisfaction scores, treatment completion rates, preventive care metrics
  • Annual benchmarking: compare Vxtra network outcomes to regional and national averages
  • Transparency: physicians see the same data employers see — no hidden metrics

3. Quarterly Physician Meetings Structured meetings in each market, every quarter.

  • Review network performance data
  • Discuss patient care patterns and opportunities
  • Address any operational issues or improvement suggestions
  • Introduce new employers and projected patient panel growth
  • Guest speaker or educational component (CME-eligible when possible)

4. Peer Referral Recognition Physicians who refer other physicians to the network are acknowledged.

  • Personal thank-you from Larry
  • Recognition at quarterly meetings
  • Priority consideration for new patient panel assignments
  • No financial incentive (inappropriate for physician relationships) — recognition and respect are the currency

5. Administrative Burden Monitoring Track and actively reduce the administrative burden on Vxtra physicians compared to their traditional insurance experience.

  • Quarterly survey: “How does working with Vxtra compare to working with [traditional insurer]?”
  • Track metrics: time from treatment decision to treatment delivery, claims processing speed, number of administrative interactions required per patient
  • Set improvement targets and report progress to physicians

6. Practice Growth Support Help Vxtra physicians grow their practices through the network.

  • As new employers join, expand physician patient panels
  • Provide market data on community healthcare needs and gaps
  • Facilitate connections between primary care physicians and specialists within the network
  • Support practice marketing to Vxtra employer employees (co-branded materials, directory listing)

C7. Key Messaging by Specialty

Not all physicians have the same pain points. While the core message (autonomy, no pre-auths, independent practice) resonates universally, the emphasis should shift based on specialty.

Primary Care (Family Medicine / Internal Medicine) — FIRST PRIORITY

Primary pain point: Volume pressure and administrative burden. Primary care physicians spend the most time on pre-authorizations because they manage the broadest range of conditions and referrals.

Key messages: - “Spend more time with each patient — not on paperwork” - “Your referrals go directly to trusted local specialists — no insurance gatekeeper” - “Build a real panel of patients from local employers who stay with you long-term” - “Vxtra patients are not cycling through on 36-month insurance rotations — they are your community”

Pitch emphasis: Patient relationships, reduced administrative burden, referral autonomy, stable patient panel from local employers.

Orthopedics / Musculoskeletal — HIGH VALUE

Primary pain point: Pre-authorizations for imaging (MRI, CT) and surgical procedures. Step therapy protocols that force non-surgical treatment before approving what the physician already knows is necessary.

Key messages: - “You determine when a patient needs an MRI — not an insurance reviewer” - “Independent orthopedic practice costs 40-70% less than hospital-based — Vxtra proves that to employers” - “No step therapy requirements dictating your treatment sequence”

Pitch emphasis: Immediate clinical authority, cost advantage of independent practice, direct relationship with employer (who sees the cost savings).

Cardiology — HIGH VALUE

Primary pain point: Diagnostic pre-authorizations (stress tests, echocardiograms, catheterizations) and medication pre-authorizations (newer cardiac medications frequently denied or delayed).

Key messages: - “Order the diagnostic workup your patient needs — today” - “No prior authorization for cardiac imaging or stress testing” - “Transparent data means you see what your patients need and act immediately”

Pitch emphasis: Speed of diagnostic decision-making, no delays in treatment, technology advantage (transparent data).

OB/GYN — ESSENTIAL FOR FAMILIES

Primary pain point: Maternity care bundling and billing complexity, pre-authorization for procedures, and loss of continuity when patients change insurance plans mid-pregnancy.

Key messages: - “Your patients stay with you through their entire care journey” - “No insurance plan changes disrupting continuity of care” - “Local employers want their employees to see physicians they trust for the most important moments”

Pitch emphasis: Continuity of care, patient-physician trust, community connection.

General Surgery — HIGH VALUE

Primary pain point: Pre-authorizations for surgical procedures, insurance-mandated delays, and cost pressure from hospital-based competitors.

Key messages: - “You determine when surgery is appropriate — not an insurance algorithm” - “Your independent surgical practice is the cost-effective option — Vxtra helps employers see that” - “Faster path from diagnosis to treatment”

Pitch emphasis: Surgical autonomy, cost advantage over hospital, faster treatment timelines.

Urology — LARRY’S SPECIALTY

Primary pain point: Pre-authorizations for imaging and procedures, referral complexity, hospital consolidation pressure.

Key messages: - “Larry built a urology practice from 30 to 100 physicians — he understands your world” - “The physician who built this network practiced in your specialty” - “Independent urology has a cost advantage — Vxtra makes that visible to employers”

Pitch emphasis: Larry’s personal credibility, shared specialty understanding, cost transparency.
