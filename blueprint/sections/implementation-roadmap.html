Section B: Technical Scope

Requirements for Vxtra Health’s digital infrastructure, from website through CRM through custom tools.

1. Website

Attribute Detail
Description Full website refresh with StoryBrand-structured copy, four persona landing pages, interactive tools, blog/resource hub. Mobile-first responsive design.
Platform Recommendation Static HTML + lightweight CMS for blog (e.g., Hugo, Eleventy, or Astro with a headless CMS like Contentful or simple markdown-based approach). Vxtra does not need a full WordPress installation. Static sites are faster, more secure, and cheaper to host. If the team prefers a traditional CMS for ease of editing, a managed WordPress installation with a premium theme is the fallback.
Hosting Netlify or Vercel (for static) or managed WordPress host (WP Engine, Flywheel). SSL included. CDN for performance.
SSL Required. Included with modern hosting. HTTPS everywhere.
Analytics Google Analytics 4, HubSpot tracking code, Google Tag Manager for centralized tag management.
SEO XML sitemap, robots.txt, structured data markup (Organization, LocalBusiness), meta titles/descriptions per page, Open Graph tags, fast page load (<3s), clean URL structure.
Responsive Design Mobile-first. Breakpoints for mobile, tablet, desktop. All interactive tools must work on mobile.
ADA Compliance WCAG 2.1 Level AA. Alt text on all images, keyboard navigation, sufficient color contrast, screen reader compatibility, form labels. Healthcare audience makes this non-negotiable.
Two-Track Routing Augusta/Gainesville visitors see local broker CTAs and market-specific content. Outside-market visitors see general consultation flow. Implementation: geo-IP detection or simple “Select Your Market” modal.
Priority Must Have
Complexity Medium
Dependencies Finalized brand copy (done), design direction (confirmed: minimalist, corporate, Apple-style), headshots (Larry’s needed), HubSpot form integrations

3. Qualification Tool

Attribute Detail
Description Interactive website widget where an employer enters basic census data to determine if they qualify for Vxtra Health. No personal employee information is collected.
Inputs Number of employees on health plan, years self-insured, company location/market, current renewal month.
Logic Minimum 100 employees on plan AND self-insured for 2+ years AND located in or near an active Vxtra market.
Output — Qualified “Great news — your company qualifies for a Vxtra Health analysis.” CTA to schedule a call. Data pushed to HubSpot as qualified lead.
Output — Not Yet Qualified Friendly, non-rejecting message. “Vxtra Health is currently available for self-insured employers with 100+ employees in select markets. Want to be notified when we expand?” Email capture for future pipeline.
Technical Approach Client-side form with simple JavaScript logic. No backend processing needed for MVP. Form data submitted to HubSpot via embedded form or API.
Priority Must Have
Complexity Low
Dependencies Website live, HubSpot form integration, qualification criteria confirmed

4. Cost Calculator

Attribute Detail
Description Simple compounding cost visualizer for employers. Shows current health insurance cost trajectory vs. Vxtra projected savings over 5 and 10 years. Uses dollar amounts, not percentages (per Larry’s directive).
Inputs Current annual health insurance spend, number of employees, current annual increase rate (default: 8%).
Output Side-by-side visualization: Line chart or bar chart showing Year 1 through Year 10. Left line: current trajectory compounding at 7-10%. Right line: Vxtra projected trajectory. Dollar difference highlighted per year and cumulative. Example: “$2M annual spend at 8% = $4.3M in 10 years. With Vxtra: $2.8M. You keep $1.5M.”
Technical Approach Client-side JavaScript with Chart.js or similar lightweight library. No backend needed. Responsive for mobile.
Design Clean, minimal. Dark background option matching brand. Large dollar figures. No jargon. Optional: “Share this analysis” button that generates a simple PDF or screenshot.
Priority Must Have
Complexity Low-Medium
Dependencies Savings projection assumptions approved by Larry, website live, design specs

Fractional CMO Recommendation

We recommend a fractional CMO / marketing partnership model over a full-time marketing hire for the first 12-18 months. Here is why:

  1. Cost efficiency. A full-time VP of Marketing or CMO costs $150K-$250K+ in salary and benefits. A fractional engagement delivers senior strategic leadership at a fraction of that cost — critical when working capital is $2M and every dollar needs to drive toward 500 enrolled employees.

  2. Immediate capability. There is no 3-month ramp-up period. Business Builders already has full context from the StoryBrand workshop, understands the messaging, knows the personas, and has the production team ready to execute. Day one is productive.

  3. Full team, not one person. A fractional model through Business Builders provides a strategist, designer, developer, copywriter, and video coordinator — not a single marketing generalist trying to do everything alone.

  4. Flexibility. As Vxtra grows and funding scales (private placement $3.5M in Q3, additional markets in 2027), the engagement can scale up or transition to an in-house hire supported by the agency. The fractional model bridges the gap.

  5. Sandra’s bandwidth. Larry acknowledged that Sandra is running full-time operations and no one on the Vxtra team is dedicated to marketing. A fractional partner handles execution so the Vxtra team can focus on what they do best — building the health plan and serving employers.

The right time to evaluate a full-time marketing hire is after Augusta has proven the model (Q1 2027) and Gainesville launch planning demands dedicated in-house coordination.
